# Company Intel — Executive Signal Refresh Example

All companies, executives, quotes, figures, and URLs are **fictional**, using the same surrogate
universe as the SKILL.md examples. This shows the **rerun pattern**: the user previously ran
`company-intel` on Helix Motion Systems (the baseline excerpts appear in SKILL.md's Examples
section, dated June 2026), and now asks again a quarter later. The agent has the prior output in
context, so it defaults to delta-first reporting — no "refresh" keyword needed.

**Trigger:** "Run company-intel on Helix Motion Systems again — earnings were last week."

---

## What's Changed Since June 2026

### 1. The digital twin program has a name, a P&L owner — and a smaller scope
- **Then:** volume hiring for digital-twin engineers and IoT platform architects across the Diversified Industrial segment; inference was that hiring ran ahead of organizational readiness.
- **Now:** the program surfaced on the Q3 call as "Helix Foresight," assigned to the Aftermarket Services unit — "Foresight is an aftermarket offering first; we'll expand from our installed base rather than chase greenfield" (CFO, Q3 earnings call, Oct 2026, transcript URL).
- **Reading:** the platform ambition got fenced into the services P&L — the segment-based org structure won, as the baseline inferred it would. For competitors: greenfield industrial-IoT deals just got less contested — **Inference**.
- **Confidence:** high (named program, named owner, direct quote)

### 2. Hydrogen language shifted from products to partnerships
- **Then:** patent clustering in hydrogen fuel-cell components; R&D outpacing product announcements — read as a decarbonization bet.
- **Now:** two co-development agreements with OEMs announced; the CEO described hydrogen as "a partnered path to market" (investor day, Sep 2026, URL). Patent filings in the class continue — **Fact** (filings index).
- **Reading:** the bet continues but the go-to-market changed — component supplier, not system seller. The competitive threat model shifts from "new rival product line" to "their components inside our OEMs' machines" — **Inference**.
- **Confidence:** medium (two data points, one quarter)

## Dropped Language
- **"Segment digital scorecards"** — mentioned on four consecutive earnings calls through Q2; absent from the Q3 script and slides entirely. Paired with the Foresight re-scoping, the internal digital-metrics push looks quietly retired — a transformation program that died without a press release.
- **"Platform"** — appeared 11 times in the Q2 materials, twice in Q3 (both times about Foresight). The vocabulary contraction matches the scope contraction; the narrative shift *preceded* any org announcement, which is why earnings materials get read as strategy documents.

## Section 9 delta (Strategic Signals)
- **Leadership:** the VP of Digital Transformation (hired 2025 from a platform-first rival) departed in September — four months after the strategy she embodied was re-scoped — **Fact** (announcement URL). Consistent with the "senior leader exits within 6 months of a strategy shift" HUMINT chain: the strategy is in trouble, or already re-decided.
- **Hiring:** digital-twin postings down from ~40 to 6, now all within Aftermarket Services — **Fact** (job boards, Oct 2026).
- **Patents:** hydrogen filings steady; no new digital-platform classifications this quarter.

## Sections with no material change
Sections 2 (revenue model), 3 (customers), 5 (product lines), 7 (competitors): no significant
change since June 2026. Second consecutive quiet quarter for Section 5 — for a company this
acquisitive, sustained product-line quiet is itself worth one watchlist line.

## Why this example works

- **It diffs; it doesn't regenerate.** Eleven sections existed in the baseline; the refresh leads
  with two material shifts, a Dropped Language read, and one delta-focused section — plus an
  honest "no change" list. The reader spends three minutes and knows exactly what moved.
- **Dropped Language carries the headline.** Nothing Helix *said* announced the digital
  retrenchment — the scorecard vocabulary vanishing and "platform" contracting 11→2 told the story
  a quarter before the VP's departure confirmed it. What leaders stop saying is the signal.
- **The Then/Now form keeps the baseline honest.** Each shift quotes what the prior run recorded,
  so the inference chain (June: "hiring ahead of readiness" → October: "the org structure won") is
  auditable rather than retconned.
- **Quiet got graded, not padded.** Four sections report no change in one line each — and one
  sustained quiet is flagged as a signal of its own.
