card serves includes the *systems integrator* (the channel is in the room), and pricing evidence

# Battle Card: Northfield Retrofit Monitoring vs Helix Foresight
**As-of date:** 2026-11-10
**Deal context:** mixed-vendor plants, 100-500 employees; integrator-led deals; plant manager + corporate engineering in the room

## 1. Thirty-Second Read
- **They win when:** the plant is predominantly Helix equipment — Foresight on their own components is credible and channel-backed — **Fact** ([compatibility docs](https://example.com/helix/foresight-docs), Nov 2026)
- **We win when:** the floor is mixed-vendor — Foresight's own docs scope it to Helix equipment, and their CFO scoped the program to their installed base — **Fact** ([docs](https://example.com/helix/foresight-docs); [Q3 transcript](https://example.com/helix/q3-transcript))
- **The one thing to say:** "Walk the floor and count the nameplates — we monitor all of them; ask what Foresight covers."

## 2. Say This
- "Foresight is scoped to Helix equipment — their own compatibility list says so" — **Fact** ([docs](https://example.com/helix/foresight-docs), Nov 2026)
- "Their CFO told investors Foresight expands 'from our installed base rather than chase greenfield' — mixed floors are not their plan" — **Fact**, exact quote ([Q3 transcript](https://example.com/helix/q3-transcript), Oct 2026)
- "Our retrofit ships with the machinery-safety documentation pack — your compliance file builds itself during commissioning" — **Fact** ([our product docs](https://example.com/northfield/docs)); no competitor lists an equivalent — **Fact** (feature absence, all three checked Nov 2026)
- (For the integrator:) "White-label the dashboards; the plant sees your brand" — **Fact** ([partner program page](https://example.com/northfield/partners))

## 3. Ask This (trap questions)
- "Ask what happens to Foresight coverage on your non-Helix lines" — documented answer: not on the compatibility list — **Fact** ([docs](https://example.com/helix/foresight-docs))
- "Ask whether Foresight monitoring requires a Helix service contract" — documented answer: bundled with aftermarket agreements per the program page — **Fact** ([Foresight page](https://example.com/helix/foresight), Nov 2026)

## 4. Watch Out For
- **Their distributor and service network is real and beloved** — do not attack Helix component quality or their channel; the integrator in the room probably resells them — **Fact** (channel structure; deal-loss debriefs)
- **Likely counter:** "Northfield is a software company; who services the sensors at 2 a.m.?" — **Inference** (their services-depth positioning). Response: the integrator services it — that's the model, and it's why the integrator is in the room.

## 5. Pricing & Packaging Snapshot
- Helix publishes no Foresight list pricing; program page ties it to aftermarket service agreements — **Fact** ([page](https://example.com/helix/foresight), as of Nov 10, 2026); deal anecdotes suggest per-asset subscription framing — **Inference** (two win/loss debriefs; small sample, labeled)
- Ours: published per-line pricing with integrator margin schedule — **Fact** ([pricing](https://example.com/northfield/pricing))
- Where "contact sales" hides the number: their multi-plant corporate agreements — unknowable from outside; do not guess in the room — **Assumption territory, flagged**

## 6. Do Not Say
- Don't say "Helix is abandoning digital" — they re-scoped it, they didn't kill it; overclaiming invites a correction from any buyer who reads transcripts — (basis: watch report, Nov 2026)
- Don't quote a Foresight price — none is published; a guessed number detonates if the Helix rep is in the follow-up meeting — (do-not-invent list)
- Don't promise coverage of *every* legacy protocol — our own compatibility matrix has gaps; hand the integrator the matrix instead — **Fact** ([our docs](https://example.com/northfield/docs))

- Integrator margin schedule is competitive with their Helix resale economics

## Why this example works

- **The channel is a card audience, not just a deal factor.** One talking point and the entire
  servicing counter exist *for the integrator* — in an integrator-led market, a card that only
  arms your own rep misses the person who actually closes.
- **Opaque pricing handled honestly.** No list price exists, so the card gives the rep the
  documented framing, labels the per-asset read as a two-debrief Inference, and explicitly forbids
  guessing — the industrial version of the do-not-invent discipline.
- **The strongest ammunition is the competitor's own words** — a compatibility list and an exact
  CFO quote. Nothing in Say This requires the buyer to trust Northfield's opinion of Helix.
- **"Do Not Say" prunes the overclaim** ("abandoning digital") that the watch report's nuance
  would otherwise decay into by the third sales retelling — cards protect reps from their own
  team's simplifications.
