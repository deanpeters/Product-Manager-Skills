# Voice-of-Customer Miner — Worked Example (Industrial)

All companies, quotes, URLs, and figures are **fictional**, including the verbatims (declared
fiction, per the sibling example's rule). Industrial sibling of [`sample.md`](sample.md), and the
harder case on purpose: industrial buyers barely post reviews. This run shows what honest mining
looks like when public voice is *thin* — smaller numbers, wider bias notes, and a heavier lean on
the one channel where practitioners actually talk.

**Invocation:** `Mine voice-of-customer for Helix Foresight and retrofit monitoring generally —
informs whether Northfield's integrator pitch leads with coverage or with compliance paperwork.
Open sweep.`

# Voice-of-Customer Snapshot

## 1. Scope
**Products mined:** Helix Foresight; retrofit monitoring category
**Decision supported:** whether the integrator pitch leads with mixed-vendor coverage or compliance documentation
**Sources swept:** plant-maintenance forum (skew: hands-on maintenance leads, candid, small-N), two B2B review sites (skew: thin in this category — 14 reviews total across vendors), a controls-engineering subreddit (skew: integrators and engineers, vendor-cynical), trade-association webinar Q&A logs (skew: corporate engineering, polite)
**As-of date:** 2026-11-24

## 2. Need Themes

### Theme: one screen for a floor full of nameplates
- **Frequency:** recurring — the forum's most-replied monitoring thread plus repeated subreddit mentions; total distinct voices ~12 (thin category; stated honestly)
- **Verbatim:** "we have four vendors' gear on one line and four dashboards. nobody looks at four dashboards" — [maintenance forum, URL]
- **Verbatim:** "the Foresight demo was slick until I asked about our non-Helix presses" — [subreddit thread, URL]
- **Who says it:** maintenance leads and integrators — **Fact** (forum roles displayed)
- **Reading:** mixed-vendor coverage isn't a feature preference, it's the daily reality the category ignores — **Inference**; corroborates the snapshot's structural read from the buyer side.

### Theme: prove the retrofit won't stop the line
- **Frequency:** recurring — appears in every channel, including the polite webinar Q&A
- **Verbatim:** "my last vendor's 'two-day install' cost us a weekend shift. never again" — [forum, URL]
- **Verbatim:** (webinar Q&A) "what's the realistic commissioning window for a running line?" — [Q&A log, URL]
- **Who says it:** plant managers and maintenance leads — **Fact**
- **Reading:** installation risk outranks feature comparisons; case-study evidence of non-disruptive commissioning may be worth more than any capability claim — **Inference**

### Theme: the compliance binder nobody has time to build
- **Frequency:** concentrated — one long forum thread on the new documentation rules, high engagement but a single venue
- **Verbatim:** "the new docs requirement means somebody's weekend disappears every time we touch a control circuit" — [forum thread, URL]
- **Who says it:** maintenance leads at smaller plants — **Inference** (self-described, unverified)
- **Reading:** supports the documentation-automation wedge, but single-venue concentration caps confidence — pressure-test in integrator interviews before it leads the pitch.

## 3. Competitor Weak Points
- **Helix Foresight:** mixed-vendor blindness, in buyers' words ("asked about our non-Helix presses") — recurring across two channels — [URLs]
- **Sensor startups (category):** "great dashboard, then the pilot ended and nobody bought sensors for the other nine lines" — pilot-to-scale gap named by an integrator — isolated but vivid; watch, don't weaponize — [subreddit, URL]

## 4. Switching Triggers
- **Push:** a botched install or a line-down event attributed to the vendor — the forum's switch stories all start with downtime — **Inference** (5 narratives)
- **Pull:** the integrator's recommendation — no switch story in the sweep begins with an end-buyer's independent research — **Inference** (absence, noted)

## 5. So What?
- **Opportunity hypotheses:**
  1. Plants consolidate on whoever shows one screen across all nameplates — **Inference**, confidence: medium-high (recurring, multi-channel, small-N)
  2. Non-disruptive commissioning evidence beats feature claims in this market — **Inference**, confidence: medium
  3. Compliance documentation is a lead-worthy wedge — **Inference**, confidence: low (single-venue concentration)
- **Battle-card-ready weaknesses:**
  1. Foresight mixed-vendor blindness in customer words — evidence quality: medium (thin N, but consistent with their own docs — triangulated Fact)
  2. Sensor-startup pilot-to-scale gap — evidence quality: low (one voice; do not put on a card yet)
- **Assumptions to validate in real interviews:**
  1. The four-dashboards pain drives budget, not just complaints
  2. Commissioning-risk fear is decision-level for the *corporate* buyer too, or only the plant
  3. The compliance thread generalizes beyond one forum's demographics

## Why this example works

- **Thin voice, handled honestly.** ~12 distinct voices would be a rounding error in SaaS review
  mining; here it's the market. The example counts voices, names venue concentration, and caps
  confidence accordingly — instead of inflating a sparse sweep into false certainty.
- **Answer to "which decision?" came back reversed from the temptation.** The user hoped
  compliance could lead the pitch; the mining says coverage leads (multi-channel, recurring) and
  compliance supports (single-venue) — public voice re-ordering the pitch is the skill earning
  its run time.
- **Triangulation upgraded one weakness.** The mixed-vendor complaint is thin on its own, but it
  agrees with Foresight's own compatibility docs — customer voice plus vendor documentation is
  what makes it battle-card-grade despite the small N.
- **The channel structure showed up in the switching triggers** — no switch story starts without
  an integrator. Even the VoC layer keeps pointing at the same strategic fact the whole
  industrial chain orbits.
